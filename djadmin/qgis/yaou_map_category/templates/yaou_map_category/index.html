The Eurasian continent, also known as the Eurasian continent, is a combination of the Asian and European continents, with an area of over 50 million square kilometers. From the perspective of plate tectonics, the Eurasian continent is composed of the Eurasian Plate, the Indian Plate, the Arabian Plate, and the North American Plate, where East Siberia is located. Selenge River Basin Geo-Spatial Data Visualization System

The Selenga River flows through the rivers of Mongolia and central eastern Russia, also known as the "Xueliangge River" or "Xuelingge River". Originating from the Hangai Mountains in Mongolia, it is formed by the confluence of the Ider River and the Mulun River. The river flows northeast and merges with the Erhun River at Sukhbaatar; The following is called the Selenge River, which continues northward and turns east into the territory of Russia, reaching the capital of Buryat, Ulan Ude. The river flows northward from here to Tatorovo, then turns westward and flows through a delta before flowing into Lake Baikal. The Selenge River flows gently downwards from Sukhbaatar to its mouth, serving as a passage for Mongolia to enter the Asian plain of Russia. The Trans Siberian Railway runs along the upper reaches of the river valley to Ulan Ude, where a branch line extends to Sukhbaatar and then up the Orhun River to reach Ulan Bator in Mongolia.
